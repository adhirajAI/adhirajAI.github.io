Research Positioning

Compact token interfaces for multimodal foundation models

Neural tokenization and sequence compression

My work develops neural tokenizers and sequence compressors that transform continuous sensory signals or long foundation-model token streams into compact symbolic representations for prediction, generation, reasoning, adaptation, retrieval, and efficient downstream modeling.

PairAlign-inspired autoregressive tokenization

PairAlign formulates tokenization as conditional sequence generation: an encoder conditions an autoregressive decoder to emit ultra-low-bit-rate token streams while preserving information needed for retrieval, prediction, and downstream modeling.

Codec-native audio intelligence

CodecSep studies neural audio codec latents as computational operating spaces, showing that source-compositional structure can be extracted directly through latent masking. This supports compute-efficient codes-in / latent- processing / codes-out systems for downstream edge/server audio processing, and more broadly motivates structured latent spaces as inspectable interfaces for reliable multimodal foundation models.
